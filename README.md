## Business context

For the past decade, the rise of large language models has been fueled by one abundant resource: publicly available text. From books and Wikipedia to open web pages, this reservoir has been the training ground for GPT-3, PaLM, Llama 3, and dozens of other models. But as the chart above shows, that stockpile is finite --- and it's running out faster than many realize.

The blue line in the chart shows the growth in dataset sizes used to train major language models. The green line represents the total effective stock of human-generated public text. By extrapolating both trends, researchers project that models will consume nearly all of this stock by the late 2020s.

- Around 2027, models will begin "overtraining," reusing much of the same text multiple times. - By 2028, the median projection suggests that the entire stock of public data will have been effectively consumed.
